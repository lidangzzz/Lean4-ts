# AI in Computing Research: A Georgia Tech Perspective

## Machine Learning Systems

Georgia Tech has established itself as a leader in machine learning systems research, with faculty and research groups focusing on the fundamental challenges of building scalable, efficient, and reliable ML infrastructure. The Institute for Data Engineering and Science (IDEaS) serves as a hub for interdisciplinary research, bringing together experts from computer science, statistics, and domain sciences to develop next-generation ML systems. Key research areas include distributed machine learning, AutoML (automated machine learning), federated learning systems, and the development of robust ML pipelines that can handle real-world data challenges. Georgia Tech researchers have made significant contributions to frameworks for scalable deep learning, optimization algorithms for large-scale training, and systems that bridge the gap between ML research and production deployment.

## AI Hardware and Computing Innovation

Georgia Tech's Center for Research into Novel Computing Hierarchies (CRNCH) represents the institute's commitment to reimagining computing architectures for the AI era. As traditional von Neumann architectures face fundamental limitations in supporting increasingly complex AI workloads, CRNCH researchers explore neuromorphic computing, in-memory computing, and specialized AI accelerators that can deliver orders of magnitude improvements in energy efficiency and performance. The School of Electrical and Computer Engineering collaborates closely with industry partners to develop next-generation AI chips, including research into analog computing for neural networks, photonic computing architectures, and 3D-stacked memory technologies. Georgia Tech's unique position as both a leading research university and a partner with major technology companies enables rapid translation of hardware innovations from laboratory prototypes to commercial applications.

## Computing Innovation and Future Directions

Looking ahead, Georgia Tech's computing research agenda emphasizes the co-design of hardware and software systems optimized for AI workloads. The convergence of AI and high-performance computing (HPC) represents a particularly active area, with researchers developing techniques to train ever-larger models while managing computational costs and environmental impact. Georgia Tech is also at the forefront of research into AI for scientific computing, where machine learning techniques are being integrated with traditional simulation methods to accelerate discovery in materials science, drug design, and climate modeling. The institute's commitment to ethical AI development is reflected in research programs that address fairness, interpretability, and the societal implications of AI systems. Through initiatives like the AI for Humanity initiative and partnerships with government agencies, Georgia Tech ensures that its computing innovations serve broader societal goals while maintaining scientific excellence.
