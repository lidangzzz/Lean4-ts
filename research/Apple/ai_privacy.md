# AI Privacy and On-Device Machine Learning

At Apple, privacy is a fundamental human right, and this principle shapes every aspect of our approach to artificial intelligence and machine learning. We believe that users should not have to choose between powerful AI features and their privacy. Our strategy centers on performing as much computation as possible directly on the device, leveraging the custom Neural Engine in Apple Silicon to enable sophisticated machine learning without user data ever leaving the iPhone, iPad, or Mac. This on-device processing model ensures that personal data--whether photos, messages, health information, or Siri interactions--remains under the user's control. Features like Face ID, on-device Siri speech recognition, and iOS photo categorization all operate locally, with raw data never transmitted to Apple servers.

To further advance privacy-preserving AI, we pioneered the use of differential privacy in our products, adding carefully calibrated mathematical noise to data before it is aggregated for analysis. This technique allows us to improve services like QuickType suggestions, Siri suggestions, and Health trends while making it mathematically impossible to trace any data point back to an individual user. We open-sourced our differential privacy framework to advance the field and demonstrate our commitment to transparency. Beyond differential privacy, we employ techniques such as federated learning, where models are trained across decentralized devices holding local data without the raw data ever being exchanged, and secure multi-party computation for scenarios requiring server-side processing.

The introduction of Apple Intelligence in 2024 marked a significant evolution in our privacy-preserving AI strategy. Private Cloud Compute extends on-device processing to the cloud for more complex AI tasks while maintaining our privacy guarantees. Requests sent to Private Cloud Compute are encrypted end-to-end, processed in isolated enclaves, and immediately deleted after use--with independent researchers able to verify these claims through our verifiable transparency logging. Apple Intelligence features like Writing Tools, image generation, and enhanced Siri capabilities demonstrate that cutting-edge generative AI can be delivered without compromising user privacy. We continue to invest heavily in privacy-preserving machine learning research, including techniques like homomorphic encryption and secure enclaves, ensuring that as AI becomes more capable, it also becomes more private.
