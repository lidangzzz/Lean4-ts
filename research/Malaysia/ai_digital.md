# AI in Digital Economy: Malaysia's Perspective

## Digital Transformation

Malaysia has positioned itself as a regional leader in digital transformation through its comprehensive MyDIGITAL Economy Blueprint, launched in February 2021 with the ambitious goal of transforming the nation into a digitally-enabled, high-income country by 2030. The blueprint is built on five strategic pillars: digital infrastructure, digital government, digital economy, digital skills, and digital society. Under this framework, Malaysia has made significant strides in expanding broadband connectivity through the JENDELA (National Digital Network) initiative, developing smart city projects in major urban centers, and promoting widespread digital adoption among small and medium enterprises (SMEs). The Malaysia Digital Economy Corporation (MDEC) continues to spearhead initiatives to attract foreign digital investments while nurturing local tech talent and startups, with the digital economy targeted to contribute 22.6% to the national GDP by 2025.

## AI Policy and Governance

Malaysia's approach to artificial intelligence governance is anchored in the National Artificial Intelligence Roadmap (2021-2025), developed under the Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation (MOSTI). This roadmap emphasizes responsible AI development across key sectors including manufacturing, healthcare, agriculture, and smart cities. Malaysia has established AI Innovation Centres nationwide and has positioned MIMOS, the national applied research and development center, as a key driver of AI research. The country is actively developing ethical AI guidelines aligned with international standards while ensuring AI technologies serve national interests. Malaysia participates actively in regional AI governance discussions through ASEAN frameworks and has committed to building AI capabilities that respect data privacy, promote transparency, and ensure equitable benefits across all segments of society.

## Malaysia's AI Roadmap

The National AI Roadmap outlines Malaysia's strategic vision to become a competitive AI hub in Southeast Asia by focusing on talent development, research excellence, and industry adoption. Key initiatives include establishing AI Centers of Excellence in universities, creating specialized AI talent programs to produce 20,000 AI professionals by 2025, and developing industry-specific AI solutions for priority sectors such as manufacturing (Industry 4.0), precision agriculture, and healthcare diagnostics. The roadmap also emphasizes public-private partnerships to accelerate AI innovation, with collaborations between government agencies, multinational technology companies, and local startups. Malaysia aims to leverage its multicultural workforce and strategic location to attract AI investments while developing indigenous AI solutions tailored to regional needs. The government has committed significant funding through various digital grants and incentive programs to support AI research, startup development, and enterprise digitalization efforts.
