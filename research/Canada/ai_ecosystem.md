# Building a National AI Ecosystem: Canada's Perspective

Canada has established itself as a global pioneer in artificial intelligence through its Pan-Canadian Artificial Intelligence Strategy, launched in 2017 as the world's first national AI strategy. Administered by CIFAR (Canadian Institute for Advanced Research), the strategy supports three flagship National AI Institutes: Amii in Edmonton (Alberta Machine Intelligence Institute), Mila in Montreal (Quebec Artificial Intelligence Institute, founded by deep learning pioneer Yoshua Bengio), and the Vector Institute in Toronto. These institutes bring together over 1,200 researchers and more than 125 Canada CIFAR AI Chairs conducting cutting-edge research in areas including AI safety, drug discovery, machine learning for health, autonomous systems, and natural language processing. Canada ranks first in the G7 for year-over-year growth of AI talent and has published the most AI-related papers per capita in the G7 every year since 2019. In 2022-23, there were over 140,000 actively engaged AI professionals in Canada, a 29% increase from the previous year.

Talent development forms a cornerstone of Canada's AI strategy, with programs spanning from high school students to postdoctoral fellows. CIFAR and its partners provide training opportunities to hundreds of Canadian and international students annually through initiatives such as the Deep Learning + Reinforcement Learning Summer School, AI4Good Summer Lab, and the Next Generation AI Programs dedicated to engaging equity-deserving groups. The Canada CIFAR AI Chairs Program attracts global talent and trains the next generation of AI leaders. Budget 2024 introduced significant enhancements to research support, increasing master's scholarships to $27,000, doctoral awards to $40,000, and post-doctoral fellowships to $70,000 annually. Additionally, $50 million over four years was allocated to provide skills training for workers in sectors disrupted by AI, ensuring the workforce adapts to the changing economy.

Government support for AI in Canada has been substantial and sustained. Since 2017, the federal government has invested over $2 billion toward AI, and Budget 2024 announced a monumental $2.4 billion increase in targeted AI support. This includes $2 billion for computing infrastructure and AI capabilities through the new AI Compute Access Fund, $200 million to support AI start-ups in bringing new technologies to market through the Canadian AI Sovereign Compute Strategy, $50 million for the Canadian AI Safety Institute (CAISI) to ensure safe and trustworthy AI systems, and $5.1 million for an AI and data commissioner to enforce the Artificial Intelligence and Data Act. The government is also investing $200 million through Regional Development Agencies to help businesses adopt AI across the country, ensuring that Canada's AI ecosystem continues to drive innovation, economic growth, and job creation.
