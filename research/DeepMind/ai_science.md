# AI for Scientific Discovery

At DeepMind, we believe artificial intelligence represents a transformative tool for accelerating scientific discovery across biology, physics, chemistry, and beyond. Our flagship achievement, AlphaFold, demonstrated this vision by solving the 50-year-old grand challenge of protein structure prediction. By accurately predicting 3D protein structures from amino acid sequences, AlphaFold has enabled researchers worldwide to understand disease mechanisms, design novel enzymes, and accelerate drug discovery. The AlphaFold Protein Structure Database, containing over 200 million predicted structures, has become an essential resource for the scientific community, fundamentally changing how biologists approach structural questions.

Beyond biology, we have applied AI to fundamental challenges in physics and mathematics. Our work on tensor networks and quantum computing has shown that machine learning can discover efficient representations of complex quantum states, potentially accelerating quantum chemistry simulations and materials science research. Systems like AlphaTensor have discovered novel, efficient algorithms for matrix multiplication—a foundational operation in computing—demonstrating that AI can contribute to theoretical computer science itself. In plasma physics, our AI controllers have achieved breakthrough results in tokamak fusion reactors, helping to stabilize the volatile plasma conditions necessary for sustainable fusion energy, a critical step toward addressing global energy challenges.

The convergence of AI with scientific simulation opens unprecedented opportunities for discovery. We are developing AI systems that can propose hypotheses, design experiments, and interpret results across chemistry and materials science. Our graph neural networks have predicted properties of molecules and materials with remarkable accuracy, enabling rapid screening of candidates for batteries, catalysts, and pharmaceuticals. By combining deep learning with physics-based simulations, we aim to create AI scientists that can navigate vast chemical and physical search spaces, identifying promising directions that human researchers might never consider. This human-AI collaboration represents the future of scientific inquiry—one where machine learning amplifies human creativity and accelerates our understanding of the natural world.
