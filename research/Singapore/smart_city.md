# AI in Smart Cities and Urban Planning: Singapore's Perspective

## The Smart Nation Initiative

Singapore's Smart Nation initiative, launched in 2014 and led by the Ministry of Digital Development and Information, represents one of the world's most comprehensive national digital transformation efforts. Ranked 9th in the 2025 IMD Smart City Index, 2nd in the 2025 IMD World Digital Competitiveness Ranking, and 3rd in the 2024 UN E-Government Development Index, Singapore has established itself as a global leader in leveraging technology for urban solutions. The initiative is built on three core pillars: creating a Smart Nation that citizens can trust, one that helps people grow, and one that keeps the community together. This whole-of-nation approach integrates digital identity systems (Singpass), cashless payment infrastructure, smart urban mobility solutions, and comprehensive digital government services to create a seamless citizen experience.

## National AI Strategy 2.0 and Urban AI Applications

The National AI Strategy 2.0 (NAIS 2.0), launched in December 2023, exemplifies Singapore's commitment to "AI for the Public Good, for Singapore and the World." The strategy focuses on widespread AI adoption while pursuing excellence in AI development and deployment across multiple sectors. In urban planning, Singapore deploys sophisticated AI applications including the Integrated Environmental Modeller, which simulates micro-climate conditions such as wind, temperature, sunlight, and noise to transform town planning and design. Smart City solutions combine data, sensors, and automation across three key areas: Lived Experience (automated immigration using biometrics that reduce checkpoint wait times by 40%, barrier-free access routing for wheelchair users, and smart parking systems), Productivity (autonomous mobile robots for logistics and healthcare, drones for infrastructure inspection, and 5G networks enabling ultra-fast connectivity), and Urban Sustainability (the Open Digital Platform in Punggol Digital District for real-time monitoring and resource optimization, and smart water meters for efficient utility management).

## Digital Governance and Responsible AI

Singapore has emerged as a global pioneer in AI governance and digital ethics. The AI Verify Testing Framework, first released in 2022 and recently updated to include Generative AI applications, helps companies assess responsible AI implementation against 11 internationally recognized AI governance principles. Project Moonshot, one of the world's first Large Language Model evaluation toolkits, provides open-source solutions combining benchmarking and red-teaming capabilities for testing LLMs. Internationally, Singapore works closely with key partners including the United States through the US-Singapore Critical and Emerging Technology Dialogue to shape global AI governance norms. The government has driven broad-based AI adoption across public agencies, developing tools that empower public officers to leverage AI in daily work while improving AI literacy through training programs. Over 50 companies across various sectors have established AI Centres of Excellence in Singapore, and the $150 million Enterprise Compute Initiative supports companies' AI transformation by facilitating access to cutting-edge AI tools, cloud compute, and engineering support. Singapore hosted the AI Research Week in January 2026 alongside the 40th AAAI Conference, further cementing its role as a global AI hub.
