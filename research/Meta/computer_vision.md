# AI in Computer Vision and Augmented Reality: Meta's Perspective

Meta has established itself as a leader in computer vision and augmented reality through our pioneering work on the Segment Anything Model (SAM), DINO, and other foundational vision models. SAM represents a breakthrough in image segmentation, enabling users to segment any object in any image with just a click or prompt, demonstrating the power of promptable segmentation. Our DINO and DINOv2 self-supervised learning approaches have set new benchmarks in visual feature extraction, enabling robust visual understanding without requiring labeled data. These models form the backbone of our computer vision infrastructure, powering everything from content understanding on Facebook and Instagram to the perceptual systems in our AR glasses and Quest VR headsets.

In the realm of AR and VR, Meta's Reality Labs is pushing the boundaries of what's possible with AI-driven experiences. Our Quest headsets leverage advanced hand tracking, eye tracking, and spatial mapping powered by real-time computer vision algorithms. We're developing neural input technologies like EMG wristbands that translate subtle muscle movements into digital interactions. Our AR glasses initiative combines compact display technology with AI assistants that can understand and interact with the physical world around you. Technologies like Passthrough API on Quest 3 and the upcoming Meta Quest 3S enable seamless mixed reality experiences where virtual content intelligently interacts with real-world environments, all powered by sophisticated depth sensing and scene understanding models.

The metaverse represents the convergence of our AI and spatial computing efforts, where computer vision serves as the perceptual foundation for immersive experiences. We're building AI systems that can understand context, recognize objects and people, and enable natural interactions within virtual spaces. Our Codec Avatars research uses machine learning to create photorealistic digital representations of people for social VR, requiring advanced facial capture and reconstruction. We're also investing heavily in generative AI for 3D content creation, enabling users to generate virtual environments and objects through natural language prompts. As we build toward more immersive metaverse experiences, our vision models will continue to evolve, enabling richer understanding of both physical and virtual worlds while maintaining our commitment to privacy-preserving AI that processes sensitive visual data on-device whenever possible.
