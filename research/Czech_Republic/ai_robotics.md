# AI in Robotics and Automation: Czech Republic Perspective

## Industrial Robotics Leadership

The Czech Republic stands as one of Europe's most advanced nations in industrial robotics and automation, with a manufacturing heritage deeply rooted in its historical role as the industrial heartland of the former Czechoslovakia. As the birthplace of the word "robot" (coined by Czech writer Karel Capek in his 1920 play R.U.R.), the country maintains a symbolic and practical leadership in robotics innovation. Czech manufacturing facilities, particularly in the automotive sector, boast one of the highest robot densities in the European Union, with companies like Skoda Auto leading the integration of AI-powered robotic systems. The nation's manufacturing sector has embraced Industry 4.0 principles, implementing collaborative robots (cobots), autonomous guided vehicles (AGVs), and smart manufacturing systems that leverage machine learning for predictive maintenance, quality control, and adaptive production processes.

## Manufacturing AI and Innovation Ecosystem

Czech universities and research institutions have established themselves as significant contributors to AI-driven automation research. The Czech Technical University in Prague (CVUT) and Brno University of Technology operate specialized robotics laboratories focusing on human-robot interaction, computer vision for industrial inspection, and autonomous systems. The country's strong industrial base has attracted significant investments from global automation companies, while domestic firms like KUKA Robotics Czech Republic and local AI startups develop specialized solutions for manufacturing optimization, supply chain management, and robotic process automation. The Czech government's National AI Strategy, launched in 2019, has prioritized robotics and automation as key focus areas, supporting research initiatives and public-private partnerships aimed at maintaining the country's competitive edge in advanced manufacturing. Additionally, the Czech Republic's strategic location in Central Europe positions it as a hub for automation technology transfer between Western and Eastern European markets, with Prague and Brno emerging as centers for AI and robotics innovation clusters.
