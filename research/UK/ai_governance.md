# AI Governance and Policy Frameworks: United Kingdom

## National AI Strategy and Regulatory Approach

The United Kingdom has established itself as a global leader in AI governance through its pro-innovation regulatory framework, articulated in the 2023 AI Safety Summit and the subsequent establishment of the AI Safety Institute. Rather than implementing sweeping legislation like the EU AI Act, the UK has adopted a context-specific, principles-based approach that empowers existing sector regulators—the Financial Conduct Authority, the Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency, and others—to oversee AI within their respective domains. This flexible framework is built on five cross-sectoral principles: safety, security, and robustness; appropriate transparency and explainability; fairness; accountability and governance; and contestability and redress. The government's "pro-innovation" stance aims to balance the economic benefits of AI adoption with appropriate safeguards, positioning the UK as a hub for responsible AI development while maintaining competitive advantage in the post-Brexit regulatory landscape.

## International Cooperation and AI Safety Leadership

Internationally, the UK has taken a prominent role in fostering multilateral cooperation on AI governance, most notably through hosting the landmark Bletchley Park AI Safety Summit in November 2023, which brought together government leaders, tech executives, and civil society from 28 countries. The summit resulted in the Bletchley Declaration, committing signatories—including the US, China, and EU member states—to collaborate on identifying and mitigating risks from frontier AI systems. The UK's AI Safety Institute, the world's first state-backed body dedicated to AI safety testing, works in partnership with its US counterpart and conducts pre-deployment evaluations of advanced AI models. Additionally, the UK remains an active participant in global forums such as the G7 Hiroshima AI Process, the OECD AI Policy Observatory, and the Council of Europe's Framework Convention on AI, while continuing to align closely with the United States through bilateral agreements on AI safety standards and information sharing.
