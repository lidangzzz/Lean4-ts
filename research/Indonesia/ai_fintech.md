# AI in Financial Technology: Indonesia's Perspective

Indonesia, Southeast Asia's largest economy with over 270 million people, has emerged as a dynamic fintech powerhouse driven by rapid digital adoption and a young, tech-savvy population. The country's fintech landscape has experienced explosive growth, with the number of fintech companies increasing from around 150 in 2018 to over 400 by 2025. This growth is fueled by high smartphone penetration, improving internet infrastructure, and a significant unbanked population of approximately 66% that presents immense market potential. The Indonesian government, through the Financial Services Authority (OJK) and Bank Indonesia, has actively supported fintech innovation through regulatory sandboxes and progressive policies, creating a conducive environment for AI-driven financial solutions to flourish.

Digital payments form the backbone of Indonesia's fintech revolution, with AI playing an increasingly central role in enhancing security, personalization, and accessibility. E-wallets like GoPay, OVO, Dana, and ShopeePay have transformed how Indonesians transact, processing billions of dollars in transactions annually. AI algorithms power real-time fraud detection systems, analyzing transaction patterns across millions of users to identify suspicious activities with unprecedented accuracy. Additionally, AI-driven credit scoring models are revolutionizing lending by analyzing alternative data points such as mobile usage, social media behavior, and e-commerce transactions to extend credit to previously underserved populations. This approach has enabled fintech lenders like Kredivo, Akulaku, and Investree to disburse loans to millions of customers who lacked traditional credit histories.

Indonesian banks are aggressively embracing AI to stay competitive and meet evolving customer expectations in the digital age. Major banks like Bank Central Asia (BCA), Bank Mandiri, and Bank Rakyat Indonesia (BRI) have invested heavily in AI-powered chatbots, virtual assistants, and personalized recommendation engines to enhance customer service and engagement. BCA's virtual assistant, Tanya BCA, handles millions of customer inquiries monthly using natural language processing, while Bank Mandiri's AI systems analyze customer data to offer tailored financial products. AI is also being deployed in back-office operations for risk assessment, regulatory compliance, and automated document processing. Looking ahead, Indonesia's National Strategy for Artificial Intelligence 2020-2045 positions AI in financial services as a priority area, signaling continued government support for innovation in this sector as the country works toward its vision of becoming a digital economy powerhouse in Southeast Asia.
