# AI Hardware and Edge Computing

## AI Accelerators

At Intel, we are driving the future of AI computation through our comprehensive portfolio of AI accelerators designed to meet the diverse needs of modern workloads. Our Intel Xeon Scalable processors with built-in AI acceleration capabilities, including Intel Deep Learning Boost (DL Boost) and Advanced Matrix Extensions (AMX), deliver exceptional performance for inference and training tasks directly on general-purpose CPUs. For more specialized AI workloads, our Intel Gaudi series of AI accelerators, acquired through Habana Labs, provides high-performance deep learning training solutions that compete with the industry's best, offering superior price-performance ratios for large-scale AI model training. Additionally, our Intel Arc graphics products bring AI acceleration to consumer and professional segments, enabling developers and creators to leverage GPU-accelerated AI capabilities for content creation, gaming, and professional visualization applications.

## Edge AI

Intel is at the forefront of Edge AI, bringing intelligence closer to where data is generated and decisions need to be made. Our Intel OpenVINO toolkit enables developers to optimize and deploy AI inference across Intel hardware, from edge devices to cloud environments, with a focus on computer vision, natural language processing, and generative AI workloads. We offer specialized edge AI hardware solutions, including Intel processors with integrated AI acceleration and our portfolio of vision processing units (VPUs) such as the Intel Movidius series, which enable ultra-low-power AI inference at the edge. These technologies empower industries from manufacturing and retail to healthcare and smart cities to deploy real-time AI applications with minimal latency, enhanced privacy, and reduced bandwidth requirements, all while maintaining the flexibility to scale from proof-of-concept to production deployments.

## Neuromorphic Computing

Intel is pioneering neuromorphic computing through our Loihi research platform, which represents a fundamental departure from traditional von Neumann architectures by mimicking the brain's neural structure and event-driven communication patterns. Loihi processors implement spiking neural networks (SNNs) that process information in a manner similar to biological neurons, achieving orders of magnitude improvements in energy efficiency for certain types of cognitive workloads, including pattern recognition, optimization problems, and real-time learning tasks. Through our Intel Neuromorphic Research Community (INRC), we collaborate with academic institutions, government labs, and industry partners to explore applications in robotics, autonomous systems, sensory processing, and adaptive control systems, demonstrating that neuromorphic approaches can solve complex AI problems with significantly lower power consumption than conventional deep learning methods, pointing toward a future of more efficient and brain-inspired computing architectures.
